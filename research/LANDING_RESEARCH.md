## Part 2 — Build Brief for ClickNComply Landing

### 1. Three Hero Headline Options

**Option A — The Consultant Replacement (recommended)**
> **"The compliance consultant lives in your laptop now."**
> *Subhead:* Every framework your business has to follow — GDPR, H&S, food, fire, AML, Bribery Act — handled in one place. £9/month, no consultants, no calls, no pestering. Free forever if you don't mind a watermark.

- **Pros:** Instantly conveys the consultant-replacement value. "Lives in your laptop" is sticky, slightly cheeky, low-corporate. Subhead does the universality and pricing in one breath.
- **Cons:** "Consultant" might feel American to some UK ears; could read as anti-consultant rather than pro-SME.

**Option B — The Punk Promise**
> **"Compliance, sorted. We won't pester you."**
> *Subhead:* One tool, every framework, £9/month. No sales calls, no upgrade nags, no 'jump on a quick chat'. Free if you don't mind us putting our name on the bottom.

- **Pros:** Hardest punk-ethos lean. "We won't pester you" is the differentiator stated upfront. Funny, memorable.
- **Cons:** Doesn't telegraph "universal" or "consultant-replacement" loudly enough — relies on subhead to do the work.

**Option C — The Money Hook**
> **"Save £2,000 on a compliance consultant. Pay £9 instead."**
> *Subhead:* ClickNComply handles every compliance framework your business needs. Self-serve, no calls, no upsells. Free forever with a small watermark.

- **Pros:** Pure money math — clearest direct ROI. Will out-perform A and B on cold traffic and PPC.
- **Cons:** Slightly transactional — sacrifices the brand voice for the conversion. Reads like a Google Ad headline blown up.

**Recommended:** **Option A**, with **Option C** held in reserve as the H1 for **paid-traffic landing variants** and PPC. Option A wins for organic / referral / brand searches; Option C wins for cold-funnel-bottom intent.

### 2. Recommended Page Structure (top-to-bottom)

1. **Sticky nav** — minimal: logo, "How it works", "Pricing", "Sign in", and a "Start free" button. No mega-menu.
2. **Hero** — H1 (Option A), 2-line subhead, two CTAs side-by-side: **"Start free"** (primary) and **"See it in action (60s)"** (secondary, opens an inline Loom-style demo). Right side: a real product screenshot or short looping video showing a framework being ticked off. Mobile: stack, sticky CTA bar appears on scroll.
3. **Frameworks-as-logos grid** — 24 framework badges in a 6×4 grid: GDPR, ISO 27001, H&S, RAMS, CDM, Food Hygiene, AML, Bribery Act, MHRA, FCA, ICO, etc. Header: "Every framework. One subscription." Borrows directly from Vanta's pattern. Communicates universality instantly.
4. **The "we don't bother you" manifesto block** — see section 6 below. This is the second screen, intentionally — it filters out enterprise tyre-kickers early.
5. **How it works (3 steps)** — 1) Tell us what your business does. 2) We pre-fill 70% of your compliance. 3) Tick off the rest, export as PDF/share link. Adapts ISMS.online's "81% done" hook.
6. **Live interactive demo / quiz** — "Tell us your industry, we'll show you what frameworks apply." This is the page's secret weapon: it converts curiosity into a populated dashboard before the user has signed up. Like Plausible's live dashboard, but personalised.
7. **The £2,000 saving calculator** — slider: "Number of frameworks you need." On every move, shows: "A consultant would charge ~£X. ClickNComply: £9/mo." Pure money, hard to argue with.
8. **Pricing — single card + free card** — see section 5 below.
9. **Why we built this (founder note)** — 150 words, plain text, signed. Builds the cold-start trust signal Plausible used. Honest, slightly punk, ends with a personal email address.
10. **Watermark / viral mechanic explainer** — "Free works because of our watermark. Here's what it looks like →" then an actual screenshot of a PDF footer. Address the one objection upfront.
11. **FAQ (10 items, accordion)** — see bonus deliverables.
12. **Final CTA strip** — one big box, dark background: "Start free. Upgrade to £9 when you're ready. Cancel any time. We promise we won't email you about it." + button.
13. **Footer** — slim. Logo, key links, "Made by [name] in [town], UK", a single email address (`hello@clickncomply.com`), no Twitter/X/LinkedIn icons in the primary footer — those go in a sub-row to keep the page clean.

### 3. Visual Style Recommendation

**Pick: Modern flat with hand-drawn / claymation accents.**

Reasoning: The brand voice is cheap-and-cheerful, slightly punk, trustworthy. Pure brutalism (Buttondown style) would alienate non-technical SME owners. Pure illustrated-friendly (Notion / Gusto style) would feel too corporate-cheery and lose the punk edge. The right register is **Linear-clean as the chassis, with Aardman/claymation flourishes** — small stop-motion-style illustrated characters in the hero and section breaks (the consultant ripping arms off, a clay file folder being smashed open, a clay handshake when you complete a framework).

This means:
- **Type:** A workhorse sans (Inter, Geist, or General Sans) for body. A characterful display face for the H1 — something like **Söhne** or **GT America Mono** works; or a slightly warped grotesque to lean punk.
- **Colour:** A high-contrast palette with one signature colour. Recommend **off-white background, true-black text, and a single saturated lime / acid-yellow accent** for CTAs and watermarks. Lime reads as cheap-cheerful + slightly punk (not corporate purple, not safety-orange).
- **Imagery:** 4–6 short looping clay-stop-motion clips placed sparingly: hero, "how it works" steps, the watermark explainer, the final CTA. Each loop is 2–3 seconds. Done in claymation it costs almost nothing to make in After Effects with a hand-feel filter — you do not need real Aardman.
- **Texture:** A very light paper-grain background overlay (1–3% noise) gives the cheap-cheerful texture without going maximalist.

### 4. Specific UI Patterns to Steal (with sources)

1. **Frameworks-as-logo grid in the hero zone** — Vanta does this in their compliance section ([https://www.vanta.com/products/automated-compliance](https://www.vanta.com/products/automated-compliance)). Single most efficient way to communicate "universal" without reading copy.
2. **The "X% done before you log in" hook** — ISMS.online ([https://www.isms.online](https://www.isms.online)) leads with "81% progress the moment you log in". ClickNComply: "We pre-fill 70% of your compliance pack before you've even signed up."
3. **Interactive subscriber/framework slider for pricing** — Buttondown ([https://buttondown.com/pricing?count=1000](https://buttondown.com/pricing?count=1000)) lets users drag a count slider and instantly see the price. Steal verbatim.
4. **Live embedded product dashboard on the homepage** — Plausible ([https://plausible.io](https://plausible.io)) embeds their actual analytics on their landing page — visitors can play with it. ClickNComply: embed a sample compliance dashboard for "Plumber Ltd, Sheffield" or similar — pre-populated, clickable, live.
5. **End-of-form / end-of-PDF viral CTA card** — Loom's end-card pattern. The watermark on the free PDF should be a *last-page banner* with QR + URL, not a tiny footer logo.
6. **Anti-corporate manifesto block** — Storylane ([https://www.storylane.io](https://www.storylane.io)) literally says "no sales calls, no procurement, no implementation project". ClickNComply: copy this almost verbatim, scaled to SME tone.
7. **Sticky mobile CTA bar** — Gusto's pattern. Always-visible "Start free" button at the bottom of the viewport on mobile — increases mobile conversion 15–25% per the cited research.
8. **Minimal, signed founder note instead of testimonials** — Buttondown and Plausible both do this. A single paragraph signed by a real human with a real email is better than seven fake testimonials at launch.

### 5. Pricing Section Approach

**The challenge:** £9/mo + free is unusual and could make free feel like the "real" plan. Solve by reframing it: free is the *trial*, watermark is the *cost*, £9 is the *unlock*.

**Recommended pricing block (two cards, side-by-side, equal visual weight):**

```
┌─────────────────────────────────┐  ┌─────────────────────────────────┐
│  FREE                            │  │  PRO         [Most popular]    │
│  £0 / forever                    │  │  £9 / month                    │
│                                  │  │                                │
│  Unlimited frameworks            │  │  Unlimited frameworks          │
│  Unlimited PDFs                  │  │  Unlimited PDFs                │
│  All updates                     │  │  All updates                   │
│  "Powered by ClickNComply"       │  │  Your branding only            │
│  watermark on outputs            │  │  No watermark                  │
│                                  │  │  Priority email support        │
│  [ Start free ]                  │  │  [ Start 14-day Pro trial ]    │
└─────────────────────────────────┘  └─────────────────────────────────┘
```

**Key principles:**
- **Free is genuinely powerful** — unlimited frameworks, unlimited PDFs, no feature gates other than the watermark. This is the Buttondown/Mailchimp way.
- **The watermark line is honest, not buried** — listed as a feature line, not in 8pt grey.
- **Both buttons identical visual weight** — no "DOWNGRADE" framing on free.
- **Below the cards:** the slider — "How many people in your business?" Drag it: free stays free, Pro stays £9 (since it's flat). Surfaces the *clarity* — there are no per-seat surprises. A single number, forever.
- **Below the slider:** a small "compared to" row — "A compliance consultant: £2,000–£5,000. A typical GRC platform: £400–£800/mo. ClickNComply Pro: £9/mo." Three rows, no logos named (per the no-competitor-names rule), just category labels.

### 6. The "We Don't Bother You" Section

This section, placed second on the page (right after the hero), is the **brand-defining moment**. It signals to enterprise-tyre-kickers that this is not their tool, and bonds with stressed SME owners instantly.

**Recommended copy block:**

> ## We won't bother you.
> No sales calls. No "jump on a quick chat". No upgrade nags in your inbox.
> No upsell pop-ups. No "your compliance score is dropping" anxiety emails.
> No account manager named Brad.
>
> You sign up. You do your compliance. You leave.
> If you need help, our docs are good and our support email replies within a day.
> That's it. That's the deal.
>
> [ Start free — £0 forever ]

**Visual treatment:** Plain background (off-white or deep matte black), single column, large body type (24px+), no images. The section *looks* boring on purpose — it lets the copy land. Slight clay-style icon next to the section title (a "do not disturb" sign with little clay flourishes).

**Why it works:** Anti-corporate-as-product is a strong moat. SaaS buyers in 2026 are exhausted by sales cycles. This section says "we built the thing you can buy without being sold to" — and that's both a promise and a polite filter.

### 7. Trust Signals Strategy (the cold-start problem)

You have no testimonials, no logos, no customer counts. Plausible, Buttondown, and Beehiiv all started from zero — here's their actual playbook, adapted:

1. **Replace customer logos with framework logos.** Vanta's framework grid trick. You don't need "Trusted by Microsoft" if you can show "Covers GDPR, ISO 27001, H&S, RAMS, CDM, Food Standards, AML, Bribery Act, MHRA, FCA…". The frameworks themselves do the trust work because the user recognises them as *real and authoritative*.
2. **A signed founder note.** "Hi, I'm [Name]. I built ClickNComply because I spent £4,200 on a compliance consultant in 2024 and got back a Word doc I could've made in an afternoon. So I made this. Email me if you've got questions: [hello@clickncomply.com](mailto:hello@clickncomply.com)." That single paragraph beats a fake testimonial wall every time. Plausible used a near-identical tactic for their first 18 months.
3. **"Independent and bootstrapped" badge** in the footer or trust strip — a small label that says "Built in [town], UK. No VC. No upsells. Just us." Plausible ([https://plausible.io/about](https://plausible.io/about)) uses this almost verbatim.
4. **Open the books, slightly.** A live "X people use ClickNComply right now" counter (updates from your actual user count). Even at 12, it's authentic. Ramp it as you grow. **Honesty beats inflated numbers.**
5. **Pre-emptive press / mention strip.** As soon as you get *any* mention — even a single Indie Hackers post or Reddit thread or trade-press blurb — feature it. "As mentioned on Indie Hackers" with a single screenshot is more credible than fake logos.
6. **Use the work itself as the proof.** Embed a live, anonymised compliance pack on the page — let visitors download a real one. Buttondown does this with newsletter samples. The product *being good* is the social proof.
7. **A money-back / cancel-any-time line** in 14pt: "Cancel any time, no questions, two clicks. Email export of your data is free, always." This is risk-reversal as a substitute for testimonials.
8. **"As we grow, you save."** A single line near pricing: "We'll never raise your price retroactively. £9 today is £9 forever." It signals integrity in lieu of a track record.

### 8. Mobile Considerations

SME owners (plumbers, builders, café owners, hair salons, small ecom) overwhelmingly browse on phone, often during downtime — between jobs, on a tea break, at bedtime.

**Mobile-specific patterns:**
- **Sticky bottom-CTA bar.** Always-visible "Start free" button, 56–64px tall, full-width with 16px side margins, occupying the thumb-zone. Borrowed from Gusto's pattern. Converts 15–25% better on long pages per the cited research.
- **Hero stacks, doesn't shrink.** H1 stays large (32–40px), subhead stays at 18px+. Don't crush the hero into a postage stamp. Hide the secondary "see demo" link below the primary CTA on mobile.
- **Frameworks grid → horizontal scroll carousel** with momentum, snap-to. Don't try to fit 24 logos in a 4×6 mobile grid — they become unreadable. A 1-row scrollable "frameworks supported" strip works better.
- **Slider for pricing must support touch with haptic feedback.** Use a native HTML range with restyling, not a JS-heavy widget that breaks on iOS Safari.
- **Founder note section uses a real photo + handwriting font.** Personal touch that scales down well on mobile.
- **Tap targets ≥ 44×44pt** (Apple HIG). The footer email link should open the mail client directly with `mailto:`.
- **Performance budget: <100kb JS, <200kb total CSS, hero LCP under 1.5s on 3G.** Plausible and Buttondown both optimise for this. ClickNComply's audience is on mobile data, not gigabit fibre.
- **Forms minimised.** Sign-up: email + password, that's it. No "company name", no "size of business", no "industry" — gather that *after* they're inside, in the onboarding quiz.
